Greyhound-Data Fundraising are putting together a World Group to raise funds to save greyhound racing in N.S.W. Australia. This will be an ongoing campaign and just as those who oppose greyhound racing in N.S.W. Australia continue to do so we will continue supporting the greyhounds that we love. Their duties will include contacting the greyhound participants within their state and asking them for items to donate towards our fundraising auctions. Our representatives will also be expected to keep everyone up to date via this thread. Any support from other groups striving to achieve similar results which would like to support us please email tony@greyhound-data.com Let’s all start heading in the same direction. Our first auction will take place as soon as our representatives are in place. If you would like to donate anything for our auction please post it here. Thank you for “standing up to be counted” and helping us do our bit to save Greyhound Racing in N.S.W. Australia.

Ireland

On 18th April, 1927, the first greyhound race track was opened in Ireland. This was in Celtic Park, Belfast, and was followed immediately by Shelbourne Park, Dublin one month later. In the same year, tracks were opened at Harolds Cross, Dublin and Dunmore Stadium, Belfast, to be followed in the following year by Cork.

Shortly after its introduction, the control of greyhound racing in Ireland came under the aegis of the Irish Coursing Club which supervised coursing, one of the most ancient field Sports, as well as publishing the Irish Greyhound Stud Book.

In 1958, the Government established Bord na gCon (The Irish Greyhound Board), to control, promote and operate greyhound racing in the Republic. The Irish Coursing Club continued to regulate greyhound racing in Northern Ireland.
